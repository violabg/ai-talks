# Slide Vocabulary

Layout patterns to mix and match. Not exhaustive — invent new layouts when content demands.

## Visual-first slides

- **Flowchart slide** — SVG flowchart that builds step by step with staggered animations. For processes, pipelines, decision trees.
- **Diagram slide** — relationship diagram, architecture overview, or concept map as inline SVG with animated connections.
- **Comparison slide** — two/three columns that animate in: before/after, good/bad, option A vs B.
- **Timeline slide** — horizontal or vertical progression showing evolution or sequence.

## Content slides

- **Statement slide** — single bold statement or question, centered. No bullets. Used to introduce a key idea or create a pause.
- **Code spotlight slide** — code block where specific lines highlight sequentially via animation, annotations appear alongside.
- **Quote/insight slide** — key insight from the article, typographically prominent, optional supporting context below.
- **Key points slide** — when a list of points genuinely is the best format, 3–5 short items with staggered entrance. Ask first: could this be a flowchart or diagram?

## Structural slides

- **Title slide** — article title, punchy presentation-specific subtitle, tags.
- **Section divider** — full-screen heading marking a major topic shift.
- **Closing slide** — final takeaways. Summary diagram, set of principles, or single memorable statement.
